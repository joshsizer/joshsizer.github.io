How We Do It

How do Evaporation credits work?

These credits, also known as sewer credits, allow you to get money back for water volume that evaporates from cooling towers, irrigation systems, or condensers. For the most part, water and sewer utilities charge your building based on total water consumption. If 50,000 gallons flow through your water meter, the utility companies assume that 50,000 gallons will be put back into the sewer system. This simply is not the case when using a cooling tower or condenser where a large percentage of water evaporates and does not go back into the sewer system. This billing practice creates a charge to your business for services you do not use. By monitoring and measuring these volumes, we can prove the exact water used and send those measurements to local utility companies.

What will the savings be like?

Sewer credits are often overlooked and something that companies can recover to drastically reduce operating costs associated with cooling towers, irrigation systems or condensers. Savings can range from 20% to 40% of monthly sewer charges, and help reduce sewer costs for many years. Credits are also a hedge against annual sewer rate increases.

Who can receive water credits?

We currently work with almost 200 water utilities in 35 states to secure sewer credits for commercial clients who operate cooling towers, irrigation systems or condensers. To find out if your building qualifies, send us a copy of your most recent water and sewer bill.

LEED Points

Sub-metering cooling towers can qualify for up to 2 LEED points. If your company is pursuing LEED certification, sub-metering can help you qualify, in addition to reducing your operating costs.
